## Caption (publish-ready)

November 14, 2026. One date. Three systems. No grace period.

On that day, Fedwire, CHIPS, and SWIFT all complete their move to ISO 20022 at once. After the cutover, payments carrying unstructured address data won't be downgraded or queued — they'll be rejected. Red Compass Labs calls it one of the most significant changes ever imposed on US payment infrastructure, with no option to stagger the work.

That's fewer than five months away.

The visible task is technical: structured data, richer message formats, new validation. The harder part is operational — every wire and cross-border flow that still touches legacy address formats has to be remediated, tested, and monitored before the deadline, not after it.

This is where a payment hub earns its keep. With Payment Galaxy on BankOS, banks handle ISO 20022 enrichment and validation in the orchestration layer, across rails — instead of patching each core and correspondent connection separately. One place to get the data right.

The deadline is set. The only question is whether your infrastructure is ready before it arrives.
