## Caption (publish-ready)

For years, real-time cross-border payments were a capability only the largest banks could offer. That's starting to change.

This month, SWIFT began rolling out a new retail cross-border scheme built with 40+ banks, with more than 25 institutions going live by the end of June across corridors to 11 countries — offering what SWIFT describes as cost certainty, full principal delivery, and end-to-end tracking for consumer and SME flows (SWIFT; American Banker). Bank of America has said it will launch its own real-time cross-border solution for commercial clients next quarter (Bank of America, June 4, 2026).

The pressure that used to sit at the top of the market is moving down — to regional and community banks whose corporate clients will soon expect the same experience.

The catch: you can't bolt real-time cross-border onto legacy rails one corridor at a time. Trade Galaxy and Payment Galaxy on BankOS connect domestic rails (FedNow, RTP, ACH) and cross-border flows in a single hub, so banks can route money intelligently — without a bespoke build for every corridor.

Cross-border is becoming table stakes. Infrastructure is what decides who can actually deliver it.
