## Caption (publish-ready)

When the IMF publishes a research note on a technology, it has left the hype cycle and entered the infrastructure conversation.

That's where agentic AI in payments now sits. A 2026 IMF research note describes AI agents that can initiate, route, reconcile, and handle exceptions across payment flows with delegated authority — not a chatbot bolted onto online banking, but a decision layer sitting on top of the rails (IMF Notes 2026/004). Oracle extended its agentic AI platform to corporate banking in April 2026 — a signal that institutional-grade agentic infrastructure is no longer just an experiment.

Here's the part banks miss: an AI agent is only as capable as the data and rail access you give it. Point one at a tangle of legacy integrations and batch files, and there's nothing useful for it to act on.

This is the case for an API-first, multi-rail foundation. BankOS gives banks a composable layer above their existing core, and Payment Galaxy gives agents clean, real-time access to payment rails — so AI has something real to work with, without a core replacement.

AI isn't the hard part anymore. The infrastructure underneath it is.
