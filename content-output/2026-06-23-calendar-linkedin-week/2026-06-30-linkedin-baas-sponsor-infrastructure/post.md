## Caption (publish-ready)

The "BaaS" label took a beating after a wave of enforcement actions. Sponsor banking itself? Quietly growing.

The number of US sponsor banks has grown, even as regulators raised the bar on what a compliant program looks like (The Financial Brand, 2026). The market is consolidating around institutions that can treat fintech partners with the same diligence as a commercial lending relationship — and back it with infrastructure that holds up to scrutiny.

That's the real shift. Winning embedded-finance relationships in 2026 is less about being first and more about being able to demonstrate real-time account visibility, multi-rail payments, and monitoring designed to support compliance programs — not batch-era systems stitched together after the fact.

This is what composable infrastructure is for. With Account Galaxy and Payment Galaxy on BankOS, a bank can stand up an embedded-banking program — real-time ledger, multi-rail disbursements, monitoring designed to support oversight — without rebuilding its core. The compliance bar went up; the infrastructure bar went up with it.

Embedded finance rewards the prepared, not the early.
