## Caption (publish-ready)

Tokenized deposits have moved from the whiteboard to production.

JPMorgan's Kinexys platform is reported to be processing billions of dollars a day in wholesale tokenized-deposit transactions, and several of the UK's largest banks are running live pilots through mid-2026 — production, not proofs of concept (industry reporting, 2026). This month's GENIUS Act proposed rulemaking takes a notable position, too: tokenized deposits would remain FDIC-insured regardless of the technology used to represent them — a potential resolution to a long-standing question for banks weighing the move. The rules aren't final yet.

For banks, the strategic question isn't whether tokenized money is real. It's where it lives.

Run tokenized deposits and stablecoins on a system separate from your fiat ledger, and you've built reconciliation risk into the foundation — two sources of truth that have to agree at settlement. Tokenizing money without a unified ledger is how reconciliation breaks at 2 a.m.

Token Galaxy on BankOS gives banks one ledger for fiat and tokenized money, so both settle and reconcile in the same place. Finzly provides the infrastructure to move and record the money — banks stay the issuers, custodians, and decision-makers.

Is your ledger ready to handle fiat and tokenized money as one?
