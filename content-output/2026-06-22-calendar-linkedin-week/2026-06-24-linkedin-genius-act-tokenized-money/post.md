## Caption (publish-ready)

The GENIUS Act gave the U.S. its first federal framework for payment stablecoins. The final rules from six agencies are expected by July 18 (Chapman and Cutler rulemaking tracker).

For banks, the countdown isn't really about stablecoins. It's about ledgers.

Any institution that wants to custody, facilitate, or settle tokenized money will have to move it alongside fiat — and reconcile both. Do that on two disconnected systems and you've built operational risk into the foundation. Tokenizing money without a unified ledger is how reconciliation breaks at 2 a.m.

Token Galaxy on BankOS gives banks one ledger for fiat and tokenized money, so stablecoin and dollar flows settle and reconcile in the same place. Finzly provides the infrastructure to move the money — banks stay the issuers, custodians, and decision-makers.

The framework is almost final. The infrastructure question lands on your desk regardless of how the rules read.
