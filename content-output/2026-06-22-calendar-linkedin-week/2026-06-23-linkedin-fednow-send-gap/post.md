## Caption (publish-ready)

More than 1,700 financial institutions have joined FedNow (Federal Reserve). Most of them still can't send.

Receive-only was a fine place to start. It's a risky place to stay.

In a joint survey by the Faster Payments Council and Finzly, 73.4% of institutions cited moderate-to-severe challenges with legacy systems when it comes to originating instant payments. The capability gap isn't strategy — it's plumbing.

Meanwhile demand is climbing: the FedNow Service processed 2.73 million payments worth $271 billion in Q1 2026, up 10.6% quarter over quarter (Federal Reserve). Corporate clients who can receive instantly now expect to pay instantly too.

This is exactly the gap Payment Galaxy is built to close. It activates two-way send across FedNow, RTP, ACH, and wires through one hub on BankOS — plugging into your existing core instead of replacing it.

Being able to send isn't a premium feature anymore. It's the baseline your clients are measuring you against.
