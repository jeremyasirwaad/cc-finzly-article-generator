## Caption (publish-ready)

Today, Nacha's fraud-monitoring rules expand to cover every non-consumer ACH participant.

The instinct is to read that as "real-time payments are risky." The data says otherwise.

PYMNTS Intelligence reports that 85% of banks expect fraud to rise as instant payments scale — yet checks remain far more likely to be lost, stolen, or altered than electronic transfers. The fear is running ahead of the reality.

Here's the real exposure: fragmented payment data. When ACH, wire, RTP, and FedNow each live in their own silo, fraud teams can't see the whole picture — and neither can the monitoring the new rules now require.

A unified payment hub changes the math. Finzly's Payment Galaxy runs every rail through one orchestration layer on BankOS, so monitoring, controls, and reporting work from a single, real-time view of money movement — without replacing your core.

The banks that unify their payment data don't just meet the mandate — they get a clearer shot at the fraud everyone's worried about.
