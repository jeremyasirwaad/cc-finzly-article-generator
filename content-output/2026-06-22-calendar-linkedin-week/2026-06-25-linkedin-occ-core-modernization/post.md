## Caption (publish-ready)

Core replacement used to be a technology project. Increasingly, it's a governance event.

Regulators are treating an active core transformation as an elevated operational-risk event — expecting documented governance, tested rollback, and vendor controls under the examiner's eye.

That changes the math on "rip and replace."

The pressure to modernize is real. ABA Banking Journal reports that 21% of bank data is duplicated and 28% of legacy applications remain undocumented (April 2026) — debt that quietly undermines every AI and digital initiative built on top of it.

But big-bang replacement isn't the only path forward. BankOS is built to modernize around your core, not through it — adding capability module by module with the Galaxy suite, so you're never betting the bank on a single cutover.

Modernization shouldn't read like a risk disclosure. Composable, incremental change keeps progress and prudence on the same side.
