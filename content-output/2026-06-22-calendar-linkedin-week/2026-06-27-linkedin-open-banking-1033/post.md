## Caption (publish-ready)

The open banking deadline came and went on April 1. Nothing happened. That's exactly why some banks are about to make an expensive mistake.

The CFPB's Section 1033 rule is enjoined and under reconsideration — paused, not gone (Cozen O'Connor client alert, April 2026). No new compliance date has been set. It's tempting to read that as permission to stop building.

But the direction of travel hasn't changed. Corporate clients, fintech partners, and your own digital roadmap already assume secure, permissioned data sharing. When a replacement rule lands, the banks that paused will be rebuilding under a compressed clock while everyone else is already live.

Readiness shouldn't be tied to a deadline that keeps moving. Digital Galaxy and the open API architecture of BankOS help banks build toward open banking on their own timeline — secure data sharing as a capability you control, not a fire drill you scramble through.

The regulation is paused. Client expectations are not.
